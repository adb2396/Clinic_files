Cancer Types

Cancer Types & Common Cancers

Cancer are of two types:
1. Benign : These tumours are not cancerous, they do not spread to other parts of the body. In common language we sometimes call them as only tumours.
2. Malignant: These tumours are known as cancer or carcinoma and have a tendency to spread

SARCOMA

Begins in bone, cartilage, fat, muscle, blood vessels or other connective or supportive tissue.

CARCINOMA

Begins in the skin or tissues that line or cover internal organs.

LYMPHOMA

Begins begins in lymph nodes or lymphocytes of the body.

MELANOMA

Arising from skin.

LEUKAEMIA/ BLOOD CANCER

Starts in blood-forming tissue, such as the bone marrow, and causes abnormal blood cells to be produced.

Myeloma

Multiple myeloma is cancer that begins in plasma cells, another type of immune cell in bone marrow.

Brain and Spinal Cord Tumors

These tumors are named based on the type of cell in which they formed in the central nervous system. Few examples are astrocytoma, glioma and even lymphoma.

COMMON CANCERS WORLDWIDE

In Females

  • Breast Cancer
  • Ovarian Cancer
  • Cervical Cancer (Cervix)
  • Endometrial Cancer (Uterus)

In Males

  • Prostate
  • Colon cancer
  • Germ Cell Tumour (Testicular in young males )

Common in both

  • Lung Cancer
  • Oral cancer (Tongue, Larynx)
  • Lymphoma (Hodgkins and Non Hodgkins)

Rare Cancers

  • Melanoma (skin cancer)
  • Thyroid cancer
  • Renal cancer
