Treatment of Cancer

Surgery, cancer drugs (chemotherapy) and radiation therapy (radiotherapy) are all proven to cure cancer, extend life, or improve quality of life. The type of treatment or the order of treatment will be different for individual patients, depending on the location of the tumour, the stage of the disease at diagnosis . Once the diagnosis of cancer is established, various specialist sit together and discuss the treatment plan. The plan of treatment is devised by a multidisciplinary team which is also called as tumour board/MDT. The tumour board usually comprise of

  • Medical Oncologist
  • Surgical Oncologist
  • Radiation Oncologist
  • Pathologist
  • Nuclear Medicine specialist

Treatment depends upon the symptoms, type of cancer and its stage. If the disease is localised, it can be surgically resected or irradiated locally. However, if the disease is spread to more than one part of the body then treatment modality is systemic therapy such as chemotherapy or targeted therapy. Various treatment modalities are used by cancer specialist. These modalities are not exclusive in nature. They are combined in various ways such as CHEMORADIOTHERAPY (CT+RT) where radiotherapy and chemotherapy are given concurrently.

Treatment Modalities in Oncology

Surgery
This is the prime or first modality of treatment specially for localized stage I cancer. The classical example is breast lump, tongue or oral cavity ulcer and or colon cancer. There is a concept of organ preservation in oncology where we try to give pre operative chemotherapy or radiotherapy to reduce the tumour bulk and then operate. This helps to reduce the extent of surgery and thus cause less mutilation associated with an extensive upfront surgery. There is also evolving role of minimally invasive surgery such as robotics for urological or gynecological cancers. There are several types of cancer surgery

1. Curative surgery

It simply involves removal of a cancerous tumor. It works best on localized cancers that haven't yet spread to other parts of the body, and is often followed by radiation therapy or chemotherapy to make sure all cancerous cells have been removed.
Curative surgery types include:

  • Minimally invasive surgery
  • Robotic surgery

2. Preventive surgery

It is used to prevent cancer from occurring. Many colon cancers can be prevented by removing precancerous polyps before they become malignant. A woman at very high risk for breast cancer may decide to have her breasts removed rather than worry about getting breast cancer later in life. You can remove several pre cancerous lesions of head and neck before they grow into cancer.

3. Reconstructive surgery

It returns the body to normal or near-normal appearance or function following cancer treatment. The most common restorative surgery is breast reconstruction after a mastectomy. Facial reconstruction after head and neck cancer surgery are also examples of reconstructive surgery.

4. Diagnostic surgery (biopsy):

In this procedure, the surgeon removes some or all of a tumor for examination to determine if the growth is cancerous. A biopsy can be done in several ways:

  • Fine Needle Aspiration (FNA): a needle is inserted into the tumor and material is drawn out for inspection under a microscope.
  • Incisional or excisional biopsy: the patient is put under local or regional anesthesia, which numbs the area, and a small incision is made in the skin. The surgeon either removes a piece of a large tumor (incisional), or the entire mass (excisional), for further examination. If the tumor is in the chest or abdomen, general anesthesia is used.
Radiation Therapy
Radiotherapy involves the usage of specialized X rays or Gamma Rays which are focused on the cancer/ involved body part by specialized machines in a very focused and specialized manner. There has been tremendous advance in the technology and we have now very high end machines such as tomotherapy, true beam, cyberknife to deliver Radiotherapy. There are various type of radiotherapy techniques based on their precision with which they can deliver radiation to tumour and avoid spill to normal tissues. Some of the examples are IMRT (Intensity modulated Radiotherapy), IGRT ( Image guided Radiotherapy) and SBRT( Stereotactic Radiotherapy).
Chemotherapy
Chemotherapy drugs are usually mixed in normal saline and administered through vein. Most of the intravenous chemotherapy is done in a day care set up where patients are admitted for 4 to 8 hours and discharged the same day. Certain chemotherapy drugs can be given orally or intramuscular also. It is also given intrathecal ( by lumbar puncture) for patient suffering from blood cancer or leukemia and lymphoma. It can be used pre definitive therapy where it is called NEOADJUVANT and post definitive therapy setting where we call it ADJUVANT chemotherapy. The drugs are given at regular intervals such as weekly or three weekly. It can be also administered repeatedly with the help of catheters such as PICC ( Peripherally inserted central catheter) or by CHEMOPORT.
Hormone Therapy
It is usually in the form of depot ( long acting ) injections or oral tablets which is administered at regular intervals to control the growth of tumor. This is used mainly in tumors which are driven strongly by hormone such as breast and prostate cancer. This therapy has mild side effects and can continue for years.
Targeted Therapy / Biological agents
These are drugs which work on specific targets in the tumor or human body. The well-known examples of targeted therapy are Trastuzumab ( Herceptin) for HER2 positive breast cancer and Bevacizumab (Avastin) for Ovarian and colon cancers . These drugs are available as oral tablets such as Geftinib for lung cancer with EGFR mutations or intravenous such as Trastuzumab. Majority of this class of drugs work only in those patients which have a particular mutation. For example Herceptin can only be used in females suffering from HER2 positive breast cancer and not in others. This form of therapy is also sometimes popularly called as PERSONALIZED THERAPY.
Immunotherapy
Immunotherapy is latest class of therapeutic agents added to the armamentarium of the oncology systemic therapy. These drugs enhance the immunity of the body and activate the T lymphocytes of the body (CD8 T Cell) against cancer cells. These are also given intravenous in saline very much alike chemotherapy on day care basis. The well-known drugs are Nivolumab (Opdyta) and Pembrolizumab ( Ketruda). They are approved to be used for lung cancer, head neck cancer, liver cancer, urinary bladder cancer and Hodgkin’s lymphoma. They are given at a regular interval for months to years till the disease is under control.

Side effects of Treatment

Side effects of Surgery

  • Damage to organs in the body
  • Blood loss
  • Adverse reactions to medication
  • Pain or discomfort
  • Infections
  • Pneumonia
  • Blood loss or clots

Side effects of Chemotherapy

  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue
  • Hair loss
  • Anemia
  • Infections and Low WBC count
  • Soreness of mouth and throat problems
  • Diarrhea and constipation

Side effects of Radiotherapy

  • Temporary or permanent hair loss
  • Skin irritation causing redness or sometimes blistering
  • Some darkening of skin color in the area being treated
  • Swallowing - discomfort or pain
  • Diarrhea - if the abdomen or pelvis are treated
  • Nausea (rare)
