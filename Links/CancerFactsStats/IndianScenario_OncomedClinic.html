Indian Scenario of Cancer Burden

Cancer is the second leading cause of death globally after cardiovascular diseases. Patients with cancer generally have a poorer prognosis in India, because of relatively low cancer awareness, late diagnosis, and the lack of or inequitable access to affordable curative services compared with patients in high-income countries.
The estimated number of incident cancer cases in India increased from 5,48,000 in 1990 to 10,69,000 in 2016. Crude cancer incidence rate was highest in Kerala and Mizoram, followed by Haryana and Delhi. The crude cancer death rate in India in 2016 was 61.8 per 100000, as compared with 44.2 per one lakh in 1990. Breast, Cervical and Stomach were the top three cancers in Females and Lung , oral cavity and throat were the top of the list for Indian males.
Use of tobacco products (e.g. smoking cigarettes) are the single most preventable cause of death worldwide. The many variations between the states indicate the need for state-specific approaches for cancer control.
